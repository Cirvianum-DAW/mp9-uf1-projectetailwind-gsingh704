Rasgulla is a milk-solid-based sweet from the Indian subcontinent, and a type of mithai, popular in India, Nepal, Pakistan, the Maldives, and Bangladesh, as well as Myanmar. It is also common in Mauritius, Fiji, southern and eastern Africa, Malay Peninsula, and the Caribbean countries of Trinidad and Tobago, Guyana, Suriname and Jamaica. It is made mainly from milk solids, traditionally from freshly curdled milk. It is often garnished with dried nuts such as almonds to enhance flavour.

Why does this recipe work

  1. Shape and Texture - There is no denying that, the Rasgullas are super attractive. If not convinced, just look at the photos in this post. In addition, the moist and soft texture due to the sugar syrup is just icing on the cake.
  2. Cooking Steps - Generally, the assumption is that, any premium Indian dessert recipes are comparatively complicated to prepare. Yet there are few recipes as exceptions. One such premium dessert recipe is Rasgulla Recipe.

  4. Versatile The biggest advantage of this recipe is the variations by which it can be served. It can be served chilled, warm, hot, or with other toppings. The popular toppings are vanilla ice cream or any type of creamy ice cream.

Chef Tips for Soft Rasgulla:

  1. Ingredients Quality - The quality of the milk powder or khoya is super important for this recipe. Fresh ingredients should help to get better texture and softness.
  2. Dough Kneading - Kneading can define the result of this recipe. The kneading should be done with the palm and not with fingers for gentle pressure. Knead it till it becomes lite sticky.
  3. Frying Temperature - Ghee or oil used for frying, should not be piping hot. It should be moderate in temperature so that it gets slowly and evenly.
  4. Shape & Size - Size matters. Small-sized balls are easy to shape without any cracks while frying. Particularly, if you are a beginner, start with a small size and increase it gradually.
  5. Sugar Syrup - The sugar syrup should not have even single thread consistency. It should be lite so that it can be easily absorbed.
  6. Overcrowding - Do not overcrowd while deep frying or soaking in sugar syrup. Give little space to these Rasgullas as they tend to increase in its size in each step.
  7. Practice - It is a simple and easy recipe, yet room for mistakes. I trust, each time you prepare, you would learn and rationalize from previous encounters.
